A Machine Learning based Approach for Protecting Wireless Networks Against Virtual Jamming based Denial of Service (DoS) Attacks

Co-authors : Samin Yeaser Pranto, Arnab Das Gupta, Dr. A.K.M. Ashikur Rahman

The inherent openness of wireless channels exposes wireless networks to the physical jamming problem. In addition, the virtual carrier-sensing mechanism of the family of IEEE 802.11 based MAC protocols opens up even a less expensive virtual jamming problem. A malicious node can effectively launch Denial of Service (DoS) attacks through virtual jamming inhibiting access to a large portion of a wireless network at a minimum expense of power and significantly drop aggregate throughput and traffic carrying capacity of the network. The existing solution(s) can recover the throughput to some extent. As the attackers are developing new variants of attacks day-by-day, novel and more robust mechanisms are needed to combat virtual jamming. In this paper, we propose a novel machine learning based solution that can effectively classify the malicious (i.e., jammer) and non-malicious nodes and intelligently ignore any channel allocation requests made by the malicious nodes. Finally, by presenting rigorous simulation results we show the efficacy of the proposed solution and its superiority over other solutions.

Bangladesh University of Engineering & Technology

Drunk Driver detection

This project belongs to detect any alcoholic driver for avoiding unnecessary accidents. To do this we built a system using MQ-5 gas sensor which can detect alcohol gas and glow a green signal at the back of its led. We are assuming a motor as a vehicle. Now if it sense any alcohol gas then this will be displayed on the LCD display and system will block the motor.
